How it works

A lease, but the deposit can't be held hostage.

The same four moves as a paper tenancy — only the money is guarded by a program, and every step is on the public record.

01

Tenant locks the deposit

You move the deposit into a vault PDA at the start of the lease. It leaves your wallet but reaches no one else's.

02

Lease ends, landlord may claim

Only after the lease-end date — never before — the landlord can claim part of the deposit for genuine damage.

03

Tenant accepts or disputes

Agree and the split settles instantly. Disagree and the funds freeze — safe — for an off-chain arbiter to weigh in.

04

No claim? Auto-refund

If the landlord files nothing within the grace window, anyone can trigger the full refund back to the tenant.

Try it on devnet

Verify it in 4 steps

  1. Install Phantom, then in Phantom set the network to Devnet (Settings → Developer Settings → Testnet Mode / Devnet).
  2. Grab free devnet SOL from faucet.solana.com.
  3. Click Connect Phantom (top right).
  4. Hit Try the demo escrow below to inspect a live one — or lock your own deposit.

One honest limitation

A blockchain can't inspect an apartment. It won't judge whether the carpet is really stained. So a dispute doesn't get "decided" here — it freezes the deposit, untouched, and hands off to a human arbiter. The guarantee is custody and process, not a verdict on the damage.
